CodeTape

CodeTape lives in two layers—one symbolic, one practical—both rooted in care, reciprocity, and the ethics of the commons.

Commons Open Hardware Stories Movement Sensors Field Kits

1. A Symbol

CodeTape can be written on lessons, stories, drawings, games, movement practices, recipes, and simple inventions to say:

“This is part of the commons. Use it, adapt it, share it, and leave the path better than you found it.”

It is not a contract. It is a gesture—an act of generosity instead of gatekeeping.

Anything marked with CodeTape quietly asks:

  • Do not extract.
  • Do not hoard.
  • Share with humility.
  • Honor the people and places the work came from.
  • Return more than you take.

2. A Toolkit

CodeTape is also a low-cost, community-owned framework for building open, repairable tools that listen to land, sea, and weather. It was conceived as a gift of remembrance and reciprocity, meant to be copied, rebuilt, and localized.

Anything built with CodeTape is expected to:

  • remain open-source,
  • stay in the commons,
  • be guided by local stewardship,
  • serve ecosystems and future generations.

In practice, this side of CodeTape includes:

  • Patterns – strips, paths, and story circuits
  • Schematics – micro-nodes and safe connectors
  • Classroom Kits – guides, cards, and activities
  • Materials – sustainable, low-cost options
  • Archive – sketches and evolution notes

Why Two Layers?

Because the world needs both:

  • A symbol anyone can use instantly, with no forms and no fear.
  • A toolkit communities can use to build real devices that protect and restore the places they love.

Both grow from the same soil: reciprocity, openness, stewardship, and remembrance.

How to Use CodeTape

As a symbol:

  • Write “CodeTape” on your work, optionally with
    “Offered freely as part of the commons. Adapt and share with care.”

As a toolkit:

  • Explore the patterns, schematics, and material guides in this repository.
  • Remix them for your context.
  • Share them back into the commons.
