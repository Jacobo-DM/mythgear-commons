HearthWheel

Local coordination hub for distributed care — 6 petals + 1 core.

MythGear README

What it is

HearthWheel lives in kitchens, living rooms, and shared spaces—where “weather” (needs, conditions, offers, and alerts) can move through a community without central control or extraction.

Infrastructure for care, not control.

A Fox’s Anvil Open Resilience offering.

Core + petals

Core (always present)

Hardware interface, timekeeping, local storage, encryption, connectivity, and CodeTape gateway (distributed sensing + exchange).

Petals (configurable)

Six modular functions that can be added, removed, or swapped without breaking the system. Each petal is taught through story and felt memory.

The six petals (v1)

  • Guardian — care coordination (meds/meals/water/exercise/records). No diagnosis, no dosing advice.
  • Home — household rhythms and safety cues.
  • Community — neighbor mesh, mutual aid, local alerts.
  • DiscoveryMode — curiosity prompts and learning trails.
  • RageDance — return-to-body signals and movement rituals.
  • Regenerative Gaming — cooperative quests and repair play.

Signals, not surveillance

HearthWheel treats information as signals (with scope and expiry), so old data doesn’t haunt and power doesn’t centralize.

“Weather passing through a community”

Story-first application layer: signals move like weather—arrive, inform, and fade—without becoming a feed or a platform.
